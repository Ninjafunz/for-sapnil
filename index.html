a story, finally told

for Sapnil

You might be wondering what this is about.
Or maybe you already know.

I couldn't say this out loud without losing my nerve halfway through — so I built this instead. It's a bit long. It's a bit much. But I promise, by the end, you'll know exactly why I never said it in person.

late 2022

the first time

We need to go as far back as late 2022, when I first saw you. I don't know how to describe those feelings, but all I know is — I remember you. As you walked through the pavement toward the building, my world stopped. Not really. Just hypothetically. Because all I could see was you, while everything else blended into nothing.

"It felt like lightning striking my heart — finally giving life to it."

It took a long time after that to see you again.

the puzzle piece

your name

I finally saw you again during a break, walking toward the water tap, completely unaware I existed. "That's her," I whispered to myself. You were luminous. I was ordinary — like placing a rough stone beside the moon and expecting it to shine the same way.

The only place I ever spoke to you confidently was in my dreams. In those dreams I wasn't hesitant — we talked easily, laughed easily, until morning interrupted us. But dreams are generous. Reality isn't.

And then, one day, I found out your name — "Bipana," I thought, at first. Until I learned it was actually Sapnil. When I heard your real name for the first time, it didn't feel like just a name. It felt like music — something that had always existed, but I'd only just learned how to hear it.

"Your name. That was all. And it was more than enough."
the bravest word I ever typed

one word: "hi"

I typed and deleted about fifteen different openers before finally sending: "hi." Just that. One word. It cost me forty minutes of staring anxiously at my screen. I didn't even expect a reply.

When that one "tinggg" finally rang out, I jumped with joy seeing you reply "Hajur." The joy didn't last — I hadn't planned what to say next, panicked for an hour, then sent "K gardai xau?" which never even got a reply. My first impression was ruined. I knew it.

"Those small talks were like rays of light for me. Whatever my mood was, talking to you made it better. Every single time."
the Inter-BBA Quiz Contest

the day you noticed me

I'm not sure if you remember it, but I remember that day very clearly — etched in golden lines. There was a question about who won a Grammy the previous year. I answered "Taylor Swift." And for the first time, I felt your eyes actually land on me as I said it.

I found out later she's your favorite artist — which means, technically, I have her to thank for that one accidental moment of being seen by you.

"I am, to this day, genuinely grateful to Taylor Swift from the bottom of my heart. Please don't tell her — she doesn't know the role she played in all of this."

After that day, our paths would cross now and then. Nothing major. But getting to see your smile was always enough to make my entire day.

the welcome program, near the stairs

system shutdown

You were approaching in traditional attire instead of the academy uniform, and you actually waved and smiled at me. For a moment I genuinely thought I was hallucinating. My friend took one look at my frozen face, tapped my shoulder, whispered "I'll go ahead first," and left me to sink or swim.

Whatever I'd been planning to say vanished. My brain went offline. What came out instead was: "I couldn't recognize you." That wasn't what I meant at all — I wanted to say you looked so beautiful I almost didn't recognize you. Instead it sounded like I was asking who you were. I ruined the moment, but walking up those stairs with you was still one of the best moments of my life.

the same evening

the silver crescent moon

In the auditorium, I secretly wished the boring formal speeches would last forever just so I could keep watching you anchor the stage. Then you came back in a black saree with a silver crescent moon clip in your hair, and I was lost all over again.

That night I typed: "Maybe we can dance together someday." I never sent it. I told myself, like always — I'll say it tomorrow.

"That cost me three whole years I could have spent talking to you. Laughing with you. Knowing you properly. I'll always regret that — but I will never regret a single one of the small, beautiful moments we did have."
your birthday

a bouquet for the universe

I tried sketching you again, hoping I'd do it better this time. The lines wouldn't come out right. So I decided on flowers instead. Roses felt too common. Sunflowers were everywhere. Then I remembered something you'd once posted, years ago, about one specific flower: cosmos. The caption said giving someone cosmos flowers means giving them the universe.

"If I couldn't give you my words, I would give you my universe."

I'm still embarrassed by how it went — no speech, no ceremony, I just clumsily handed it over like a delivery guy. You said "thank you." And as always, my heart betrayed me — every word I'd practiced for weeks vanished. As I turned to leave, you said "thank you" one more time. Maybe you were waiting for me to say more. I'll never know.

"Even if the delivery was clumsy, the flowers were real. And what they meant — every bit of it — was completely real too."
if you've made it this far

congratulations

This isn't even everything. I remember the days you rushed down the stairs because you were late for class. I remember when you cut your hair. I remember seeing you sitting alone in your classroom, or standing by yourself on the top-floor balcony during break time. I could go on for years describing all those moments. But let's save that for someday else.

These were the ones that mattered most. At least, to me.

If you're still here, reading this... then I think it's finally time for me to stop hiding. Stop waiting for a "tomorrow" that never comes.

the thing I've been carrying since that morning on the pavement

I've spent years running out of "tomorrows" — trapped between fear of ruining what little we had, and fear of a life full of what-ifs.

I'm not telling you this expecting anything to change, or to force an answer. I'm telling you because today is the last day I want to be brave.

Even if this collapses into silence — I needed you to know you were deeply, sincerely, and beautifully loved by someone from afar.

You are, and will always be, my favorite plot twist. Thank you for just being you.

I really, really like you — so much that I've imagined a whole life with you, even while knowing I don't have a chance.

— the boy who once said "I couldn't recognize you" instead of telling you the truth
campus has ended. our paths may never naturally cross again.

so... what happens to our story next?

I don't want to leave an unfinished promise or awkward pressure behind. The next part is entirely up to you.

Whatever you're feeling right now — take your time. When you're ready, just message me back here, in the same chat I sent you this.

whichever it is — you still know where to find me.
